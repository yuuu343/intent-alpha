## TL;DR

The recent shape of Anthropic's hiring is consistent with **a legal-domain build-out**, not
just generic enterprise sales. The pattern is too coherent to be incidental but too thin to
call a launch — hence *medium* confidence, *12–18 month* horizon.

## What the postings show

Across recent listings on Anthropic's public Greenhouse board, three signals stand out:

- **Legal-domain SMEs** (subject-matter experts) on the applied side — not just in-house
  counsel, but roles framed around "domain expertise → model behavior."
- **Trust & safety** roles tilting toward regulated-industry deployment patterns.
- **Sales / partnerships** roles with explicit "regulated industries" or "law firms" framing.

Individually each role is unremarkable. The *cluster* is what matters: applied SMEs are
how labs typically seed a domain-tuned product line.

## Why this points to a vertical, not just sales

The cleanest read on "we hired a legal SME" is "an enterprise account asked for it."
That alone wouldn't move the needle. What shifts the read is when SME-style hiring is
matched on the **research / applied** side — that's where domain expertise gets baked into
training data, evals, and product surfaces. When those two axes hire in lockstep, the
historical pattern across labs is: vertical specialization within ~12 months.

## What would change our mind

This hypothesis flips to *low* confidence if any of the following hold over the next
quarter:

- Legal-domain hiring decelerates or visibly reverts to baseline enterprise-sales pattern.
- Comparable hiring shows up across **many** unrelated verticals (healthcare, finance,
  industrial) at the same intensity — that's a generic "regulated industries" sales
  motion, not a legal-vertical thesis.
- Anthropic publishes a contradicting roadmap signal (e.g., explicit "horizontal-only"
  framing in earnings or executive remarks).

It strengthens to *high* confidence if any of the following appear:

- A legal-specific eval, benchmark, or research artifact is published.
- Posting language shifts from "legal SME" to "legal product" / "legal model lead."
- A partnership-shaped announcement with a major legal-tech incumbent.

## Caveats

- Sample size is small. We're reading public job postings, not internal roadmaps.
- Hiring leads product by months. Even if the hypothesis is right, the surface may be
  quieter and later than the hiring suggests.
- Anthropic's enterprise motion is broad; we may be over-weighting one vertical.

## How to track

We'll re-read the Greenhouse board and revisit this article on each material change in the
posting set. Resolution criteria above are the explicit fail/pass conditions.
