## TL;DR

OpenAI's public job board carries 651 open roles on 2026-04-28. The dominant
clusters are **Go To Market (134)**, **Applied AI (87)**, **Scaling (86)**,
and **Model Deployment for Business (39)** — together about **40% of all open
positions**. Safety, policy, and red-team work combined is **30 roles (4.6%)**.
The shape is no longer a research lab. It's an enterprise software vendor that
also happens to do research.

## Why this is the enterprise-vendor shape

Three patterns are diagnostic.

**Forward Deployed Engineering at global scale.** Multiple postings explicitly
use the term — `00207abc-…` Forward Deployed Software Engineer (San Francisco),
`961f6c58-…` Forward Deployed Software Engineer (Paris),
`305a4b22-…` Forward Deployed Engineer (San Francisco), and
`5bbc43df-…` Manager, Forward Deployed Engineering. "Forward Deployed
Engineering" is Palantir's signature org pattern: engineers who embed at
customer sites and stitch together integrations until the product becomes
sticky. Hiring for FDE at multi-region scale is not a research-lab move; it's
the shape an enterprise software vendor takes when it has commit revenue to
defend.

**Account Directors per region.** Posting `18f58952-…` Account Director — Tokyo
is one of many. Combined with the 134 Go To Market roles, OpenAI is staffing
the enterprise sales motion of a public-track SaaS company.

**Compute infrastructure as a product surface.** 53 keyword-matching roles in
infrastructure / scaling / capacity — including `8fb1615c-…` TPM, Compute
Infrastructure, `770d5c3f-…` Software Engineer, Frontier Clusters
Infrastructure, and `c1316397-…` Software Engineer, Agent Infrastructure.
These are the roles that exist when you're operating compute *for paying
customers*, not just for your own research runs.

The comparison point is Anthropic (PROV-001): both companies are scaling, but
the *shape* differs. Anthropic's recent hiring centres on owned infrastructure
build-out (datacenters, capex accounting, colocation counsel). OpenAI's hiring
centres on customer-facing go-to-market and product deployment. Same ambition,
different go-to-market gear.

## What would change our mind

Hypothesis flips to **low** confidence if any of these hold over two quarters:

- Forward Deployed Engineering postings are quietly closed without backfill.
- Safety / policy hiring share rises materially above ~10% of open roles
  (signalling re-prioritisation toward the lab register).
- A leadership change publicly reframes the company strategy as
  research-first / non-commercial.

Hypothesis strengthens to **high** confidence if any of these appear:

- Disclosed enterprise revenue mix exceeds consumer ChatGPT revenue.
- "Forward Deployed" expands to additional regions (Tokyo and Paris listed
  above; APAC and EMEA expansion would be the strong signal).
- A formal customer-success / Professional Services org emerges in the
  posting set.

## Caveats

- 30 safety roles is not zero; this hypothesis is about **proportion**, not
  abandonment. The lab work continues; it's just no longer the centre of
  gravity in hiring.
- OpenAI's regional GTM scale-up follows ChatGPT-Enterprise traction, not
  necessarily new strategy. Hiring is a lagging indicator of intent decided
  6–12 months ago.
- "Forward Deployed Engineering" is now a borrowed term across the AI labs;
  presence alone doesn't prove a Palantir-shape strategy. The combination
  with regional Account Directors is what does.
