## TL;DR

This dispatch reads three public data layers — 451 open roles on Anthropic's
Greenhouse on 2026-04-28, 200 recent arXiv papers in an Anthropic-author
query, and 25 public repositories under the `anthropics` GitHub
organisation — and proposes a single technical hypothesis about what
Anthropic will publish, ship, and staff over the next 12-18 months.

The hypothesis: **Anthropic's surfaces concentrate on two specific zones.**

The first is **alignment-anchored vertical AI for regulated industries**.
Across 451 open roles, **55 are alignment-coded** (19 in Safeguards, 23 with
explicit alignment vocabulary in the title, 13 in the parallel "Beneficial
Deployments" register). The vertical Account Executives in the snapshot
attach to **Federal Civilian, Academic Medical Centers, Life Sciences,
Manufacturing & Automotive, Financial Services, and Tech** — the same
industries that show up as plugin packs in the GitHub org
(`life-sciences`, `financial-services-plugins`, `knowledge-work-plugins`)
and in third-party evaluation papers (smart-grid jailbreaking benchmarks
against NERC standards, MCP security frameworks, Constitutional Classifier
analyses). The pattern across all three layers is consistent: alignment
investment functions as a *product feature* in regulated verticals where
"safety-as-feature" is what the buyer is purchasing.

The second surface is **Claude Code as an agentic-development platform**.
The `skills` repo (125,320 stars), `claude-code` (118,781 stars),
`claude-cookbooks` (41,749), and the agent-SDK fleet (Python 6,600;
TypeScript 1,349; plus seven core SDKs in Python, TypeScript, Go, Java, C#,
PHP, and Ruby) constitute an integration ecosystem of a different kind: not
vertical-anchored, but tooling-anchored. The arXiv literature confirms
ecosystem maturity, with an explicit package-management formalism for
Claude Skills (Skilldex) and a flow of papers on Claude Code permission
models and agent threat taxonomies. The hiring evidence is in the small
"Agent Prompts & Evals" team plus the broader 41-role Applied AI Architect
band that supports customer-side agentic deployments.

A third structural fact frames both. **Anthropic's compute fabric is not
self-built.** The Google relationship — investment, TPU access, distribution
through Vertex AI — is publicly documented and shapes the surfaces
Anthropic naturally addresses. Anthropic staffs Compute (11) and Software
Engineering – Infrastructure (21) at modest scale: enough to operate on
leased fabric and own selectively (PROV-001), not enough to build the
horizontal cloud surface itself. This is not a competitive observation; it
is a structural one. Compute access from a partner frees the company to
specialise on surfaces the partner does not sell directly.

This dispatch is **a technical reading**, not a strategic or investment
analysis. We do not claim Anthropic competes with, will outperform, or
should be ranked relative to any other company. Confidence is **medium**;
horizon **12-18 months**; falsification conditions are explicit in the
final section. The single load-bearing ratio: **H_align / H = 55 / 451 ≈ 0.12** — twelve
percent of Anthropic's open roles are alignment-coded (Safeguards 19,
alignment-vocabulary titles 23, Beneficial Deployments 13). Combined with
the seven vertical AE bands and the three GitHub vertical plugin packs,
this is what an alignment-anchored vertical AI surface looks like in
headcount terms. The compute fabric is supplied externally, which is what
makes the specialisation possible.

## What this dispatch is not

Before the analysis, two register notes that the rest of the piece holds
to.

This is **not a competitive comparison**. The names Google, OpenAI, and
Microsoft appear because they describe the structural environment Anthropic
operates inside — partnerships, distribution channels, adjacent
ecosystems. They do not appear as opponents to be beaten. The only
"comparison" that matters here is internal: Anthropic's papers vs its
hires vs its repos, and what their alignment as a single signal predicts.

This is **not investment commentary**. Hiring data, paper output, and
repository activity are technical signals about *what an organisation is
building*. They are not signals about valuation, share price, or any
financial outcome. We do not extrapolate from staffing to revenue, from
revenue to market position, or from market position to anything purchaseable
on a secondary market. Readers looking for that translation will find it
elsewhere; we deliberately stop one step short of it.

The reading register, throughout, is **what Anthropic the engineering
organisation will produce next**. That is a technical question, answerable
from technical data, and that is where this piece operates.

## The compute fabric Anthropic does not have to build

Three publicly-documented facts shape the analysis. We list them up front
because they reframe what counts as "specialisation" in the rest of the
piece.

**Anthropic uses Google Cloud TPUs as a primary training and serving
fabric.** The arrangement has been described publicly in both companies'
disclosures and is not a contested matter. Anthropic does not maintain its
own GPU cluster at the scale required for frontier training; it leases the
capacity from a partner. The implications for staffing are direct: the 11
Compute roles and the 21 Software Engineering – Infrastructure roles
visible in the snapshot are sized for *operating on a leased fabric, with
selective owned augmentation* (the datacenter and capex roles documented in
[PROV-001](/articles/anthropic-compute-vertical-integration-2026-04/)),
not for building a horizontal cloud platform. A company building its own
hyperscale fabric staffs compute infrastructure at one to two orders of
magnitude higher headcount; Anthropic visibly does not.

**Claude is distributed through Google Cloud Vertex AI alongside Anthropic's
direct API.** This makes Google Cloud a major commercial channel for
Anthropic's enterprise output. The vertical Account Executives in the
snapshot — Federal, Life Sciences, Healthcare, Manufacturing — overlap
substantially with the Google Cloud enterprise sales footprint, but the
*products* sold are Anthropic's. The structural read is that Anthropic and
Google occupy different rungs of the same enterprise stack: Google sells
the cloud and the channels, Anthropic sells the AI vendor surfaces that sit
on top.

**Google has a substantial investment relationship with Anthropic, and
several Anthropic researchers have prior Google / DeepMind affiliations.**
We mention this because it is publicly documented and because it shapes the
seed-author list our arXiv query uses (Yuntao Bai, for instance, has
published in both contexts). It is not the analytical claim of this piece.
The analytical claim is downstream: with the compute fabric and a major
distribution channel underwritten by a partner, Anthropic does not need to
staff for those surfaces. It can specialise.

The rest of this dispatch is about *what specialisations the public
data shows it specialising on*.

## Surface 1: alignment-anchored vertical AI for regulated industries

Across 451 open roles, 55 are alignment-coded. The breakdown:

- **19 in the Safeguards (Trust & Safety) department** — the dedicated
  organisation around model behaviour, harm reduction, and content policy.
- **23 with alignment-vocabulary titles** — including alignment researchers,
  policy engineers, harms-team analysts, and red-team roles distributed
  across other departments.
- **13 in the "Beneficial Deployments" register** — six of these are
  explicitly "Account Executive, Beneficial Deployments [Vertical]," with
  the rest being Applied AI Engineer or Applied AI Architect roles attached
  to the Beneficial Deployments line.

Beneficial Deployments is the diagnostic structure. It is not a research
team. It is a parallel commercial line within Anthropic that uses an
alignment-coded register to organise vertical-by-vertical sales, applied
engineering, and customer-facing safety work. The verticals it visibly
attaches to in the snapshot are **Life Sciences and Healthcare**
(`5111942008` Applied AI Engineer, Life Sciences (Beneficial Deployments);
`5074995008` Strategic Account Executive, Life Sciences;
`5101832008` Account Executive, Academic Medical Centers), and the
register extends to **Federal civilian work** (multiple titles in the
"Federal Civilian Sales" trigram, `5104760008` Enterprise Account
Executive, Manufacturing & Automotive sits adjacent in industry-segmented
selling, with related Federal-specific counsel and policy roles).

A reader can interpret Beneficial Deployments two ways, and we owe the
reader the choice. **(a)** It is a substantive alignment-integrated
product line — the safety work and the deployment work are technically
intertwined, and the BD-coded titles are downstream of genuine technical
integration between alignment research and customer engineering.
**(b)** It is a brand-coded sales channel — the register is the
differentiator for the procurement gate, the underlying engineering
inside BD is the same deployment engineering as elsewhere, and the
parallel safety work happens alongside rather than in integration.
Title-regex methodology cannot distinguish (a) from (b) decisively; that
requires JD body analysis or internal access (see
[/coverage/](/coverage/) for what our methodology can and cannot see).
Our reading favours (a), because the parallel staffing of Safeguards
(19), alignment-coded titles (23), and the BD-attached engineering roles
(Applied AI Engineer, Life Sciences (Beneficial Deployments), and
similar) reads as integrated rather than parallel — but we mark this as
an open empirical question. Both readings have observable consequences in
the next snapshot: under (a) we expect alignment-substantive technical
artifacts to emerge from BD work (named alignment-feature releases tied
to specific vertical deployments); under (b) we expect BD to behave like
any other sales channel and to be reorganised, renamed, or quietly
absorbed into a generic "Industries" line if the brand-coded register
stops carrying weight in the procurement gate.

The vertical AE segmentation in the snapshot, by trigram count:

- **23 enterprise account executive** — generic enterprise-AE roles
- **10 strategic account executive** — strategic-account roles
- **7 forward deployed** — explicit FDE-shape titles
- **6 enterprise account executive industries** — generic vertical AE
- **6 account executive federal** — federal vertical AE
- **6 account executive beneficial deployments** — alignment-register AE
- **5 federal civilian sales** — federal-civilian-specific
- **3 strategic account executive life sciences** — life-sciences vertical
- **6 commercial counsel** — including `5044994008` Datacenters & Construction
  and `5045003008` Colocation & Networks

A research lab does not segment its sales force across seven enterprise
verticals plus a parallel "Beneficial Deployments" commercial line. A
company selling alignment-anchored AI as a product to *regulated industries
specifically* does. The verticals are not random: Federal civilian work
requires FedRAMP and FISMA compliance; Healthcare and Life Sciences
require HIPAA and FDA-aligned controls; Financial Services require
SOC2-plus and SR 11-7 model-risk-management posture. In each of these
verticals, "the model is aligned" is not an external research virtue but a
*procurement-level product feature*. The 19-person Safeguards team and the
33 alignment-coded papers in the arXiv layer are the technical
infrastructure that makes that product feature defensible to the
procurement gate.

The GitHub layer confirms the vertical pattern. Three industry-specific
plugin packs are visible in the `anthropics` org:

- **`life-sciences`** — 322 stars
- **`financial-services-plugins`** — 7,813 stars
- **`knowledge-work-plugins`** — 11,626 stars

A plugin pack is not a research artifact. It is a *vertical sales artifact*:
the deliverable that lets an Account Executive in a customer meeting put a
runnable, vertical-specific deployment in front of a procurement committee.
The fact that Anthropic's open-source surface includes vertical packs but
no research repositories is itself a strong signal about which work the
organisation considers its commercial moat.

The arXiv layer surrounds the same surface with a measurement-phase
ecosystem. Of 200 papers in the snapshot, 76 are evaluation or benchmarking
work, and a meaningful fraction of those are third-party studies of Claude
in real deployment contexts. Representative titles:

- *Evaluating Jailbreaking Vulnerabilities in LLMs Deployed as Assistants
  for Smart Grid Operations: A Benchmark Against NERC Standards*
- *A Formal Security Framework for MCP-Based AI Agents: Threat Taxonomy,
  Verification Models, and Defense Mechanisms*
- *Trojan-Speak: Bypassing Constitutional Classifiers with No Jailbreak
  Tax via Adversarial Finetuning*
- *Cross-Session Threats in AI Agents: Benchmark, Evaluation, and
  Algorithms*

These papers are produced by outside investigators measuring
Anthropic-shipped systems against the exact regulatory frames — NERC for
smart grid, MCP threat models for agentic deployment, constitutional
robustness for content controls — that procurement committees in
regulated industries care about. The academic field is doing
Anthropic's vertical AI procurement-validation work for it. That is the
bibliographic shape of an alignment-anchored vertical AI vendor with an
established product surface, not the bibliographic shape of an emergent
research lab.

A structural contrast that sharpens the vertical reading rather than
opposing it: the same hiring-data methodology applied to OpenAI's board
([PROV-003](/articles/openai-enterprise-pivot-2026-04/)) found a Go To
Market organisation segmented by *region* — Account Director, Tokyo;
Account Director, Paris; multiple region-specific GTM titles — rather
than by industry vertical. Both companies are at similar overall
commercial-engineering allocation (Anthropic 132 + 22 = ~34% of 451;
OpenAI 134 + 87 = ~34% of 651), but the segmentation orientation
differs. OpenAI's regional segmentation is consistent with a horizontal
market across geographies; Anthropic's vertical segmentation is
consistent with a regulated-industry-specific surface where the
procurement gate is industry-coded rather than geography-coded. This is
a technical contrast between two organisational shapes, not a
competitive ranking. Both shapes are coherent responses to different
go-to-market environments, and the relevant signal for this dispatch is
that Anthropic has *chosen* the vertical-segmentation shape, which is
what we would expect if alignment-anchored regulated-vertical surfaces
are the organising thesis of the company.

The technical prediction that follows: **the headline Anthropic deployments
in the next 12-18 months will be vertical contracts in Federal,
Healthcare/Life Sciences, and Financial Services, with the alignment
register cited as the differentiated product feature in each procurement.**
The plugin packs in the GitHub org will see continued investment and
likely additional verticals (Defense, Education, possibly Energy). The
"Beneficial Deployments" line will either remain as a parallel commercial
register, formalise into a named line of business, or expand into
additional verticals — but it will not disappear.

## Surface 2: Claude Code as the agentic-development platform

The second surface is structurally different. It is not vertical-anchored;
it is tooling-anchored. The data is concentrated in the GitHub layer.

The two largest repositories in the `anthropics` org are:

- **`skills`** — 125,320 stars — a primitive for attaching modular
  capability packages to Claude Code, with hierarchical scope-based
  distribution
- **`claude-code`** — 118,781 stars — the agentic coding product itself

Underneath sit the integration infrastructure:

- **`claude-cookbooks`** — 41,749 — canonical examples for developers
- **`claude-plugins-official`** — 18,096 — first-party plugin pack
- **`claude-code-action`** — 7,329 — GitHub Actions hook for CI integration
- **`claude-agent-sdk-python`** — 6,600 — explicit agent SDK in Python
- **`claude-agent-sdk-typescript`** — 1,349 — agent SDK in TypeScript
- **`claude-code-base-action`** — 802 — lower-level CI primitive
- **`claude-desktop-buddy`** — 1,477 — desktop client

Plus the seven-language core SDK fleet (Python 3,334 / TypeScript 1,893 /
Go 1,011 / Ruby 326 / Java 299 / C# 249 / PHP 141), the CLI, and the
distribution channels (`homebrew-tap`, etc.). The aggregate developer-tooling
surface is a coherent platform, not a collection of demos.

Three observations about its structure.

**Skills (125k stars) is the agent-extensibility primitive.** The Skills
feature lets developers bundle and distribute capability packages that
Claude Code can attach to its agent loop. This is the building block of an
ecosystem play: not a single app, but a *substrate* on which third-party
work composes. The arXiv literature has caught up: the Skilldex paper in
the snapshot (*"Skilldex: A Package Manager and Registry for Agent Skill
Packages with Hierarchical Scope-Based Distribution"*) proposes an
academic formalism for the package management of Skills, which is what
happens to ecosystem primitives once they cross the relevance threshold.
The community is treating Skills as a primitive worth formalising.

**The agent SDK fleet is sized for an integration platform.** Two
explicit `claude-agent-sdk-*` packages plus a `claude-code-action` for
GitHub CI plus a `claude-code-base-action` for the lower-level CI
integration is the surface area of a platform that expects to be embedded
inside other software development workflows. The Python and TypeScript
agent SDKs in particular signal an integration primitive aimed at the two
languages that dominate AI engineering and frontend tooling respectively.

**Auto-generated SDK production is industrial-scale, not artisanal.** The
top contributors to `anthropic-sdk-typescript` are
`stainless-app[bot]` (386 commits) and `stainless-bot` (345 commits),
two automation accounts from the Stainless SDK auto-generation service.
Across the seven-language core SDK fleet, the pattern is similar. This is
the pattern of a vendor maintaining production-grade SDKs at scale, not
the pattern of a research group hand-rolling reference clients. The
combined contributor footprint across all SDKs is closer to a platform
team's output than a research org's.

The hiring side is consistent but quieter. The 41-role Applied AI
Architect band supports customer-side agentic deployments at integrator
scale, the small **Agent Prompts & Evals** team
(`5159608008` Engineering Manager, Agent Prompts & Evals;
`5107121008` Prompt Engineer, Agent Prompts & Evals;
`5122119008` Support Operations Specialist, AI Agent Management) handles
the prompt-and-evaluation pipeline for the agentic product, and the
Engineering & Design – Product department (20 roles) provides the
front-end and UX layer. Notably, the agentic-coding work is not labelled
as "agents" in titles — it is distributed across Applied AI, Software
Engineering – Infrastructure, and Engineering & Design – Product,
because the *product* it supports is Claude Code, not "an agent
research programme." This is the same vocabulary asymmetry visible in the
cross-reference table (3 jobs match the agents regex, 7 GitHub repos do):
the agentic work is shipped through Claude Code, not staffed under that
banner.

The technical prediction that follows: **Claude Code will continue to
expand as an agentic-development platform, with the Skills primitive as
the extensibility surface and the SDK fleet as the embedding surface.**
We expect (a) additional first-party Skills tied to the regulated
verticals from Surface 1 (a Healthcare Skill, a Financial Services
Skill, etc., bridging the two surfaces), (b) deeper IDE / editor
integrations beyond the current CI hooks, (c) at least one major Skills
ecosystem-management feature (versioning, signed distribution, or
permissioned scopes — the Skilldex paper telegraphs the academic
appetite for these), and (d) continued SDK-fleet expansion to additional
runtimes that enterprise customers require (likely Rust, Kotlin, and Swift
in some order). What we do *not* expect is a consumer-mass app surface;
the staffing pattern shows no consumer growth team, no consumer marketing
team beyond brand-level Marketing & Brand, and no consumer support
operations at scale.

## Diffusion velocity: the lag from paper to job

The reading above takes job postings as the primary signal, with arXiv
and GitHub as triangulation. Job postings are a *lagging* signal: the
decision to open a role precedes the posting by some weeks, the strategic
decision to staff against a thesis precedes the role decision by some
quarters, and the underlying technical work that makes the thesis viable
usually precedes everything by months or years. Reading hiring as
"future intent" is therefore reading the present-tense expression of past
commitment, and any honest dispatch built on hiring data should make that
explicit.

The cross-reference with arXiv lets us measure the lag explicitly, by
topic. For each topic regex, we compute the median publication date of
matching arXiv papers and the median `updated_at` date of matching job
postings, and report the difference:

| Topic | Jobs | Papers | Median lag (days) |
|---|---|---|---|
| post_training | 2 | 14 | +131 |
| pre_training | 5 | 7 | +129 |
| interpretability | 3 | 10 | +86 |
| security_eng | 10 | 13 | +78 |
| alignment_safety | 23 | 33 | +74 |
| agents | 3 | 46 | +46 |
| inference_serving | 12 | 10 | +45 |
| compute_infra | 6 | 1 | +226 (single-paper outlier) |
| evaluation | 0 | 76 | — |
| applied_fde | 46 | 0 | — |
| multimodal | 0 | 9 | — |
| data_quality | 0 | 1 | — |

Two diagnostic patterns surface.

**The empty rows are the most informative.** Where one of the two layers
is empty, no lag can be computed, and the absence itself is the signal.
`applied_fde` has 46 jobs and zero arXiv hits because the
forward-deployed / applied-engineering vocabulary is not what arXiv
papers talk about — the hiring layer alone shows the surface, and no
amount of literature triangulation will catch up to it. `evaluation`
runs the inverse direction: 76 papers, zero matching jobs, because
Anthropic distributes evaluation work across other roles (Applied AI
engineers, research scientists with eval responsibilities, the small
Agent Prompts & Evals team) rather than hiring under a literal
"Evaluation Engineer" title. The two zeros, taken together, refine the
headline reading: the hiring vocabulary is
*commercial-deployment vocabulary*, not *research vocabulary*. The lag
table is therefore an honest map of where each layer's vocabulary covers
ground that the other does not.

**The lag values themselves are not evenly distributed.** Topics where
Anthropic is hiring closest to the literature frontier — agents (+46d)
and inference (+45d) — are the topics where the field is moving fastest
and where the company appears to staff in near-real-time. Topics with
longer lags — post-training (+131d), pre-training (+129d),
interpretability (+86d) — are the topics where the underlying research
has been ongoing long enough that the hiring is downstream of
well-established literature. The compute-infrastructure lag (+226d) is
an outlier driven by a single matching paper from August 2025 and
should not be read as a stable estimate. Alignment-safety (+74d) and
security-engineering (+78d) sit in the middle: not real-time, but not
deeply lagged.

This is a single-snapshot calculation. The lag values will sharpen when
multiple snapshots accumulate: at that point the script can compute lag
per posting (first-seen-in-snapshot rather than `updated_at` centroid),
and we can report a distribution rather than a median. The methodology
is in `scripts/diffusion-velocity.mjs` in the repository; the raw
numbers are in `data/diffusion/anthropic/2026-04-28.json`.

## Why these two surfaces, and not others

The two surfaces above are not arbitrary. They are the surfaces a
medium-sized AI company with an external compute fabric, an alignment
investment, and a developer ecosystem can defensibly own. Three structural
constraints close off other surfaces.

**Anthropic does not staff for a consumer mass-market AI surface.** The
Marketing & Brand department (26 roles) is at the size for B2B marketing
(content, brand, partner marketing) rather than consumer growth. There are
no consumer growth roles, no app store optimisation roles, no
consumer-pipeline data science roles, and no consumer-scale support
operations roles in the snapshot. ChatGPT-shape consumer surfaces require
a different staffing pattern — one Anthropic does not have. The technical
prediction is that Anthropic will not ship a mass-market consumer AI
product at any meaningful scale in the 12-18 month window, regardless of
what the public framing might suggest about consumer ambitions.

**Anthropic does not staff for horizontal cloud product development.**
The Software Engineering – Infrastructure department (21 roles) is sized
for operating Anthropic's own training and serving needs on a leased
fabric, not for building a customer-facing cloud product. There are no
multi-tenant cloud-platform engineering roles, no cloud-billing
infrastructure roles, no cloud-region expansion roles. Anthropic operates
*on* a hyperscaler, not *as* one. The technical prediction is that the
Anthropic API surface will continue to be a model API — not a horizontal
cloud product.

**Anthropic does not open-source research artifacts.** The 25 public
repositories in the `anthropics` org are, without exception,
product / SDK / cookbook / plugin / CLI / desktop-client surfaces. There
is no open weights repository, no open training framework, no
interpretability tool release, no evaluation harness release. Whatever
research code the organisation maintains stays in private repositories.
The technical prediction is that Anthropic will not pivot to an open
research distribution model in the window — the public artifacts will
continue to be commercial and integration-facing.

What is left, after these three constraints close off three large
surfaces, is exactly the two surfaces this dispatch identifies:
alignment-anchored vertical AI for regulated industries, and Claude Code
as an agentic-development platform. The 12-18 month forecast is that
output will concentrate there.

## The 12-18 month forecast and what would change our mind

The hypothesis is medium-confidence. It is medium and not high because
hiring shape lags structural change by approximately one quarter, the
arXiv layer carries known author-name noise, and the public coverage of
Anthropic's actual deployment wins is incomplete. The forecast follows
from the data; the falsification conditions are explicit so that future
snapshots can either confirm or reject it cleanly.

**What we expect to see (Surface 1: alignment-anchored vertical AI):**

- A material public deployment announcement in **Federal civilian** work
  (HHS, Treasury, GSA, or a Department of Defense civilian agency) within
  the window.
- A **Healthcare or Life Sciences** named-customer announcement in a
  regulated context (a major academic medical centre, a top-20 pharma, or
  a national laboratory).
- A **Financial Services** named-customer announcement at a top-25 bank,
  insurer, or asset manager.
- Continued investment in the vertical plugin packs (`life-sciences`,
  `financial-services-plugins`, `knowledge-work-plugins`), with at least
  one additional vertical pack (most likely **Education**, **Defense**,
  or **Energy / Utilities**).
- The Beneficial Deployments commercial line either expands (more vertical
  AEs, possibly its own departmental name) or formalises into an explicit
  "Industries" line of business.

**What we expect to see (Surface 2: Claude Code as platform):**

- At least one major Skills ecosystem-management feature
  (signed distribution, versioning, permissioned scopes, or registry
  formalism).
- Additional first-party Skills tied to the verticals from Surface 1
  (a Healthcare Skill, a Financial Services Skill, etc.).
- Deeper IDE integrations beyond the current CI hooks — most likely a
  first-party VSCode integration extension, or a JetBrains-family
  integration.
- Expansion of the agent SDK fleet to additional languages
  (Rust, Kotlin, and Swift are the most likely next additions in some
  order).
- Continued growth in `claude-code` and `skills` repository stars,
  contributors, and dependent repository counts.

**What we do not expect to see:**

- A consumer mass-market AI app launch at meaningful scale.
- A horizontal cloud product launch.
- A frontier model open-weights release.
- A pivot away from the Google compute fabric (TPU access, Vertex AI
  distribution, the underlying investment relationship).

**The hypothesis flips to low confidence if any of the following specific
conditions hold. Each is observable from public data we can re-fetch at
the next snapshot.**

- **By 2026-12**, no publicly disclosed Federal civilian agency contract
  announcement of $10M+ scale (HHS, Treasury, GSA, civilian DoD, IRS, VA,
  or equivalent named-customer scale).
- **By 2026-12**, no Healthcare or Life Sciences named-customer
  announcement at top-25 hospital system, top-20 pharma, or top-15 life
  sciences firm scale.
- **By 2027-04** (the next snapshot at our planned cadence), fewer than
  five "Beneficial Deployments" titles in the snapshot AND no successor
  commercial line under another name (e.g., "Industries", "Mission",
  "Public Sector").
- **By 2027-04**, the count of Account Executive titles attaching to
  Federal / Life Sciences / Healthcare / Manufacturing combined drops
  below twelve (currently observed 26+, including six "Account Executive
  Federal", six "Account Executive Industries", three life-sciences
  variants, and the Manufacturing & Automotive AE).
- **By 2026-12**, Anthropic launches a consumer-app surface that reaches
  more than one million monthly active users within six months of launch
  (or equivalent app-store-scale distribution) — indicating a
  consumer-mass pivot the staffing pattern would not predict.
- **By 2027-04**, each of the three vertical plugin packs
  (`life-sciences`, `financial-services-plugins`,
  `knowledge-work-plugins`) shows zero commits across the 90-day window
  immediately preceding the snapshot.
- **Within the window**, Anthropic public statements materially reduce
  reliance on Google TPU, OR the Vertex AI Anthropic SKU is delisted,
  OR Anthropic begins staffing hyperscale fabric construction at
  two-orders-of-magnitude higher infrastructure headcount than the
  current 32 directly-relevant roles (11 Compute + 21 Software
  Engineering – Infrastructure).
- **Within the window**, public reframe of the organisation's core
  register away from "enterprise AI vendor" toward "pure research lab",
  accompanied by Sales department dropping below 25% of total headcount
