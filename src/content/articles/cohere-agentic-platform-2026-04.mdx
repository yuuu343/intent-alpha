## TL;DR

Cohere's public Ashby board on 2026-04-28 carries 115 open roles. The
department layout is unusual for a frontier-AI lab:

- **Modeling — 33 roles (28.7%)** — the research-and-training nucleus, with
  pre-training, post-training, training performance engineering, and
  training infrastructure all visible as separate sub-functions.
- **Agentic Platform — 15 roles (13.0%)** — *its own department*, not an
  internal team within a product or research org.
- Embeddings & Search (2), Inference (4), Cohere Labs (2), Modeling for
  Public Safety roles round out the technical side.

No other frontier-AI lab in this register breaks "agents" out as a peer
department to research. Anthropic's hiring (PROV-001) centres on physical
infrastructure ownership. OpenAI's (PROV-003) on enterprise productisation
and Forward-Deployed Engineering. Databricks (PROV-004) builds an "AI
platform" but with a data-platform lineage. Cohere is the only one
structurally treating agents as the **shipped artefact**.

## Why this points to deliberate dual identity

Three observations.

**Agentic Platform isn't an internal team — it's a department.** Internal
teams don't appear separately on a public job board with their own
hiring lanes. When agents are an org with their own DevRel, applied
engineers, and infrastructure, the company is communicating that this
is a product line.

**Modeling still dominates by absolute count.** 33 vs 15 = 2.2:1 in
Modeling's favour. Cohere has not abandoned the research register the
way OpenAI's hiring has trended. The two centres of gravity coexist.

**Inference is its own department too.** 4 roles, including SRE for
inference infrastructure (`8b6696e1-…`). For a model-vendor, this is the
shape of the org operating its own inference fleet, distinct from
training compute. Combined with the Modeling sub-functions (pre-training,
post-training), the full pipeline from pre-training through agent
deployment is visible as a coherent operational stack.

## What would change our mind

Hypothesis flips to **low** confidence if:
- Agentic Platform is folded back into Product or Modeling within two
  quarters (signal that it was an organisational experiment, not a bet).
- "Applied AI Engineer — Agentic Workflows" hires consolidate into
  generalist Applied roles (signals agents-as-feature, not agents-as-product).
- Modeling share drops below 20% (signal of pivot away from research toward
  productisation only).

Hypothesis strengthens to **high** confidence if:
- Agentic Platform expands to its own DevRel / Field Engineering branches
  (currently Applied-only).
- A first-class agent product is announced with public pricing distinct
  from the Cohere model API.
- Cohere Labs (the research-lab branch) grows separately from Modeling,
  formalising a research / production split inside the company.

## Caveats

- 115 open roles is a small sample for ratio claims. The 29% / 13% read
  could shift materially with one departmental hiring round.
- "Agentic Platform" as a department name is a description; we don't have
  the org-chart evidence to be sure how independently it operates.
- Cohere is privately held and headcount-disclosed sparingly. Hiring is
  the main public signal we have; this is its limitation.

## What this implies for AI

"Agents" as a department-level commitment — not a research thread,
not a product feature — signals the field is past the demo phase.
Within 12-18 months, expect agent-shaped products with their own
pricing, their own SDKs, and their own field-engineering branches.
The capability has matured enough to be operationalised as a primary
revenue surface, not as a feature attached to a chat product. For the
AI engineer this matters concretely: building on agents stops being
a research-codebase exercise and becomes a vendor-product exercise,
with the usual trade-offs (less depth of customisation, more reliable
SLAs, structured pricing). The labs that operationalise agents-as-
product first define the orchestration / tool-use abstractions that
the rest of the field will need to either compose with or compete
against.
